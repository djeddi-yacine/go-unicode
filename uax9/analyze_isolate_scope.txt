Analyzing FSI isolate scope issue:

Test case: FSI EN R (para=0)
Expected: [0, 2, 1]
Actual:   [0, 2, 3]

Breakdown:
- FSI determines direction by looking at first strong (EN), becomes LRI
- Creates level 2 isolate (even level for LTR)
- EN gets level 2 (inside isolate)
- R currently gets level 2, then implicit -> 3

Expected behavior suggests:
- R should be at level 1 (outside isolate, RTL in LTR paragraph)

Hypothesis: When FSI has no matching PDI, the isolate scope should terminate 
when encountering a strong character of the opposite direction.

For FSI (acting as LRI) with first strong EN:
- EN is LTR, inside the isolate
- R is RTL, opposite direction -> terminates isolate scope
- R should be at outer level (0), then implicit raises to 1

This would mean we need to track when to "auto-close" unmatched isolates.
